On Building Things

August 9, 2025 • 4 min read

Shipping often beats shipping big. Small projects reduce the cost of being wrong and increase the surface area for learning.

  • Pick a tiny scope. Smaller than feels comfortable.
  • Finish and publish. Then iterate publicly.
  • Capture notes as you go—future you will thank you.

Momentum compounds. The best way to get better at making things is to keep making things.
